Omelas Thesis

Does the greater good justify the suffering or torture of a minority?


The author presents a seemingly perfect utopia built on the suffering of a small child, leading readers to question if it is justified for the greater good.


The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas challenges readers to think about grey morality and the implications of choices that harm and benefit different groups of people.


The author shows a utopia built on the torture of a small child and those who cannot justify it so they simply walk away.


The author presents an exaggerated version of our world, how many beautiful things we partake in are results of the mistreatment of real people.


This story asks us to question our own morality, whether we accept the suffering of some for the happiness of most, or if we choose to walk away altogether.


This story parallels our real life. It shows how many corporations take advantage of marginalized groups of people and what we as consumers decide to do about it.


The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas presents a moral dilemma. We question whether it is practical or fruitful to reject a practice that prioritized the greater good and accept something unfamiliar because of our morals.
